123 Tables

Tables that bring people together.

Confrontational Dining

Rectangular dining tables are innately confrontational. These traditional dining tables have parallel sides and sharp corners.

These sides pit members of the table against each other; forcing them to choose between friends and creating a social hierarchy at the table.

Primal Perception

Humans prefer smooth forms.

In a study conducted by psychologists Bar and Neta in 2006, researchers found that forms with sharp edges produced a physiological fear-response in the brains of their subjects.
In contrast, smooth forms are perceived as safe and welcoming like sitting around a campfire.

In the study "Angular Versus Curved Shapes", researchers created visual stimuli (see right), some being curved and some angular. Participants more often attributed curved shapes with positive emotions.

What if a table was shaped to facilitate human interaction?

Curved forms eliminate sides altogether. Or, more accurately,
place each individual on the same side of the table.

"Can I join You?

123 tables come in 3 sizes and can be combined for any occasion. Want to join a group of friends but don't want to move all of your stuff? Just push your tables together and enjoy each other's company.
