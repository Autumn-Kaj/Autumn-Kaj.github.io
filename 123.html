123 Tables

Tables that bring people together.

Dining tables are meant to facilitate human connection.

However, not all human interactions are the same, and are never predictable. 123 Tables address a need for a flexible dining experience that can adapt to the social situation, whatever it may be.

The shapes of objects affect us emotionally.

Humans attribute curved forms with friendly personalities and positive emotions. Harsh, rigid forms are associated with negative emotions. In one study, angular shapes even caused a fear response in the brains of participants.

In the study "Angular Versus Curved Shapes", researchers created visual stimuli (see right), some being curved and some angular. Participants more often attributed curved shapes with positive emotions.

Read "Angular vs. Curved Shapes

Our furniture is constructed with straight lines and right angles.

Overall, angular shapes are viewed by the brain as negative and austere. And yet, the majority of our furniture that we interact with daily is constructed with straight lines and right angles.

Furniture doesn't have to be so geometric anymore.

The process in which furniture has been built throughout history has established straight lines and 90 degree angles as the design norm. This is a natural bi-product of subtractive manufacturing. But with 3d printing, blow moulding, and various other modern manufacturing techniques, many more organic and unique forms could be employed to create shapes that evoke positive emotions in the humans that use them.

Design Process

My approach to the design process started after my deep dive into researching form language and how the human brain reacts to certain shapes. I decided to focus on rounded, evocative shapes that could have an uplifting effect on its users. Thus began the sketching process.

123 Tables connect with graceful curves.

The 123 tables come in 3 different sizes, each sporting the same radii. These contours allow the tables to interlock and be slid together to create larger tables, enabling more diners to join the party. The organic forms promote positive emotions in users, uplifting the space into more than just a sterile dining hall. The 123 tables put the users in control of their social experience.

123 Tables adapt to the needs of the people who use them.

123 Tables offer something that many dining tables do not - flexibility . Whether dining alone or with family and friends, the tables can be combined and arranged to accommodate a multitude of situations.

123 Tables were part of "Food For Thought."

Western Washington University's Junior Class united in 2019 to rethink the modern kitchen. "Food for Thought" was an investigative project that re-imagined how people cook, dine, and connect over food.
This project was expansive and was broken up into multiple parts, where I was responsible for designing community dining tables. These tables were meant to live in a shared community kitchen, that was part of a larger co-housing complex.
