Airbnb x Western Washington University

Airbnb's User Experience team tasked WWU's UX Capstone class with creating a design that addressed Airbnb's concerns about environmental sustainability.

Meet the team.

Western Washington University's UX classes consist of students from multiple disciplines including; industrial design, graphic design, psychology, and computer science. Our multidisciplinary team worked together remotely to accomplish this design over the course of 3 months.

"Sustainable Tourism", a growing trend in the current travel industry.

According to the World Tourism Organization:

Sustainable Tourism is "Development that meets the needs of present tourists and host regions while protecting and enhancing opportunity for the future." This is inherently important to Airbnb's business and future growth, because sustainable tourism means protecting and enhancing regions that their hosts live in, and ensuring that future guests will have the same opportunities as those in the past.

Survey Questions

We designed a survey and sent it out, accumulating 24 responses total. The survey was produced using Qualtrics and focused on AirBnb, AirBnb competitors, guest travel preferences, and sustainability.

View Survey Results

Understanding the User's Perspective

Based off of the survey, research and user interviews, the team developed a User Journey and Flow. This was used as a reference throughout the design process, since putting yourself in the user's shoes can be useful in catching problems that us designers may be oblivious to. We based our imaginary user, Abigail, after an amalgamation of user qualities and inputs that we received during the research phase.
