Slate

Classroom tablets designed to keep students
focused on what is important.

Technology and School Go Great Together.

According to the National Science Board in a 2018 study, public schools in the United States are going through a "digital conversion". Teachers are increasingly using technology to supplement or replace older teaching methods, and research is proving this to have major benefits to student learning. (Information from https://nsf.gov/)

But, There are Some Setbacks.

When I interviewed elementary school teachers, many noted that it was difficult to keep students on task at their computers or ipads. It poses a problem to education when teachers need students to work on school-work but the computers have so many other functions (like games and social media!)

My mission is to design distraction-free technology specifically tailored to the classroom.

Sketching Exploration

Slate is a product designed specifically for the classroom.

Slate is a tablet, pen and smart-board system designed for the classroom. Instead of the distraction of iPads and computers in classrooms, Slate provides a streamlined, interactive classroom experience.

Power to the pen.

Slate's stylus is slim: designed to fit perfectly into children's hands. It is made out of durable, easy to clean plastic, with a magnetic strip along one side. This magnetic strip keeps pens from rolling off desks, and also serves as an inductive charging dock when placed on the tablet.

Every child gets a tablet.

Students can only access the assignments that the teacher pulls up on the Slate-Board, which keeps the class focused on the lesson, not social media.

The tablets have a home.

Slate tablets dock within the Slate Board for charging, similar to how the Slate Pens dock within the Slate Tablets. This nesting effect keeps all pieces neatly tucked away together when not in use.

Teachers Have Control

Slate keeps students focused, and teachers in control, while maintaining the sense of discovery and excitement that technology brings to students in the classroom.

Teachers can monitor students progress on assignments and class percentages from the main Slate Board in order to best facilitate learning for everyone.
